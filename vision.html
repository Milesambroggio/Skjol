The Vision

Rooted in Scandinavian spirit, built for mountain souls

Skjol means shelter -- a place of protection and warmth. It is the hearth around which mountain life gathers.

Our Philosophy

In the Nordic tradition, there is a word -- hygge -- that defies simple translation. It speaks to the art of creating warmth, of cultivating intimacy with the people and places that matter most. It is candlelight on a winter evening, a wool blanket shared between friends, the quiet satisfaction of a space that holds you well. At Skjol, this idea is not a marketing phrase. It is the foundation of every design decision.

We believe luxury should be intentional, not ostentatious. The finest materials should serve a purpose: European oak because it warms underfoot, local stone because it connects you to the land outside, floor-to-ceiling glass because the mountains deserve to be seen from every room. Scandinavian design has always understood that restraint is its own form of richness -- that what you leave out matters as much as what you put in.

Skjol brings these Nordic traditions to the Rocky Mountains, where the landscape demands the same honesty. The result is a place that feels both timeless and unmistakably of this valley -- a modern shelter for those who seek not just a home in the mountains, but a life shaped by them.

Our Approach

Design Philosophy

Light & Space

Floor-to-ceiling glass, open floor plans, and mountain views from every residence. Interiors designed to let the landscape in and give the eye room to rest.

Natural Materials

European oak, local stone, warm metals. Every surface is chosen to honor the landscape and age with grace. Design that feels rooted, not manufactured.

Intentional Living

Flexible spaces that adapt to how you actually live. No wasted square footage. Every detail earned its place through careful consideration and purpose.

The Vail Valley has consistently ranked among the strongest real estate markets in North America. Limited land supply, world-class skiing, and an increasingly year-round economy have driven appreciation rates that outpace national averages by a significant margin. For buyers who understand mountain markets, the trajectory is clear -- and Avon sits at the center of the valley's next chapter of growth.

Short-term rental demand in the Vail corridor remains robust, with premium properties commanding nightly rates that generate compelling annual returns. Skjol's design, amenity package, and location between two world-class resorts position it to capture the top tier of this market.

Comparable new construction in Vail Village and Beaver Creek starts at $2M and frequently exceeds $3M for two-bedroom residences. Skjol offers a rare entry point: luxury-grade finishes and a premier location at a price that reflects the pre-construction moment rather than the finished product. History shows that buyers who enter at this stage benefit most as the project takes shape.

This is not speculative. It is a straightforward proposition: own in a proven market, at a proven location, at the moment when value is greatest. Founders pricing will not last, and it will not return.
